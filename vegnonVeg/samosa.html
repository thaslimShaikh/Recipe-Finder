Samosa Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup green peas, boiled
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon of oil, and a pinch of salt.
    • Add water gradually and knead into a stiff yet pliable dough.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the Filling:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Next, add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté until aromatic.
    • Add the chopped green chilies and boiled peas, and cook for 2-3 minutes.
    • Now, add the mashed potatoes, coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
    • Mix well and cook for another 3-4 minutes until the filling is well combined and aromatic.
    • Remove from heat and let the filling cool completely.
  3. Shape and Fill the Samosas:
    • Divide the rested dough into small equal-sized balls.
    • Take a dough ball and roll it out into a small circle.
    • Cut the circle into two halves.
    • Take one half and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edges with a little water.
    • Fill the cone with a spoonful of the prepared filling.
    • Seal the open edge of the cone using a little water, ensuring the samosa is tightly sealed.
    • Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
  4. Fry the Samosas:
    • Heat oil for deep frying in a pan over medium heat.
    • Gently slide the prepared samosas into the hot oil and fry them in batches until golden brown and crisp.
    • Remove the fried samosas using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

Serving Tips:

Serve hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.
